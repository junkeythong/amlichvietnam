# Comparison: Vietnamese Lunar vs. Chinese Lunar Calendar

While both the Vietnamese and Chinese lunar calendars are based on similar astronomical principles, they have key differences that occasionally lead to different dates for the same events.

## 1. Time Zone Offset
The most significant difference is the standard time zone used for astronomical calculations:
- **Vietnamese Lunar Calendar**: Uses **UTC+7** (Hanoi time).
- **Chinese Lunar Calendar**: Uses **UTC+8** (Beijing time).

Because the "new moon" (which marks the start of a lunar month) can occur between 23:00 and 00:00 (11 PM - 12 AM), a one-hour difference can shift the first day of the month by one day.

## 2. The Zodiac (Twelve Earthly Branches)
Most signs are identical, except for one:
- **Vietnamese**: The 4th animal is the **Cat** (Mão).
- **Chinese**: The 4th animal is the **Rabbit** (Thỏ/Tố).

Example: 2023 was the Year of the Cat in Vietnam, but the Year of the Rabbit in China.

## 3. Leap Month Calculations
Both systems use the same method to determine leap months (based on solar terms where a lunar month contains no "Zhongqi"). However, because solar terms are also calculated based on local time, the 1-hour difference can occasionally result in a different month being designated as a leap month.

## 4. Notable Date Differences
- **Tet/Lunar New Year**: Most years they coincide. However, in years where the new moon falls between 23:00 and 00:00 in Vietnam (but 00:00 and 01:00 in China), Vietnam will celebrate Tet one day earlier.
- **Example years with differences**: 1985, 2007, 2030, 2053.
